UW-Madison’s Cool Science Image Contest

Drawn from 93 submissions, 10 still images and two videos have been selected as winners of UW-Madison’s 2016 Cool Science Image contest.

The contest is now in its sixth year and is sponsored by Madison’s Promega Corp. Open to any UW-Madison student, staff or faculty member, the contest is intended to showcase the amazing imagery scientists and others create in support of science or exploration of the natural world. This image depicts data acquired by NASA’s Airborne Visible and Infrared Imaging Spectrometer (AVIRIS) during flights over Madison, Wis. on Aug. 16, 2011. Whereas common digital cameras record bands of red, green, and blue visible light, imaging spectrometers record light in bands spanning the visible through the far infrared regions of the electromagnetic spectrum. This image is a composite of more than 200 bands of imaging spectrometer data, stitched together from multiple flights as the aircraft flew over Madison's lakes. The data have been transformed and compressed to summarize variation across the massive spectral resolution of the sensor. Features like algal greenness, photosynthetic responses, and water depth are some of the many things that could be contributing to these vivid colors. • Image by Tedward Erker - Graduate Student, Forestry and Wildlife Ecology / Steve Kochaver - Graduate Student, Forestry and Wildlife Ecology | Airborne Visible and Infrared Imaging Spectrometer Tedward Erker / Steve Kochaver
